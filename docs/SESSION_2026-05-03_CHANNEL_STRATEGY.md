# Session 2026-05-03 Channel Strategy Context

## Discussion Summary

Nikolay reviewed marketing feedback for the Telegram channel `AI bez slozhnosti`.

The marketer recommended shifting the channel toward business:

- clearer business positioning in the name/header
- profile description focused on ready AI/automation solutions for business
- a strong welcome post for cold audience
- linked discussion group
- pinned services post
- content based on recognizable business situations, pains, false beliefs and results
- more visual/video formats
- stronger headlines
- 2-3 customer-development calls with the target audience

## Decision

The direction is accepted, but not as a dry IT-services showcase.

Keep the brand idea `AI bez slozhnosti`, but add a business gravity center:

- Nikolay as a personal expert/implementer
- AI, bots and automation for small business and experts
- simple language, no technical intimidation
- content should lead to trust and consultation, not DIY self-assembly

Recommended positioning:

> Nikolay helps small business, experts and small teams implement AI, bots, automation and content systems without technical pain.

## Audience

Primary audience:

- small business owners
- experts and private practitioners
- marketers and service specialists
- small teams
- people in Russia/CIS who heard about AI but postpone implementation

They often:

- do not know where to start
- fear complexity and wasted money
- worry about poor contractors, data leaks and useless tools
- understand that automation might help, but do not actively search for a solution

## Content Strategy

Do not make every post a direct business case. Mix formats, but keep business usefulness as the center.

Suggested starting mix:

- 40% business situations and automation
- 25% simple AI tricks for work and everyday productivity
- 20% AI news and explanations in simple language
- 10% personal/backstage observations
- 5% direct offers, audits, consultations and service posts

The channel should not feel like a sales department. It should feel useful, practical and human, while repeatedly making the reader think:

> I can come to Nikolay with this task.

## Weekly Editorial Rhythm Idea

- Monday: recognizable business pain
- Tuesday: simple AI trick
- Wednesday: niche/profession automation breakdown
- Thursday: AI news explained through practical value
- Friday: myth, mistake or risk
- Saturday: personal observation or backstage
- Sunday: soft offer or mini-audit

## Prompt Direction

Prompts should not only say "write for Russia/CIS".

They should require every business-facing material to answer:

1. Who recognizes themselves in this post?
2. What illusion does the post remove?
3. Why would the reader come to Nikolay instead of doing it themselves?

Default content frame:

- recognizable client situation
- true cause of the problem
- cost of doing nothing
- how AI/bot/automation can help
- soft CTA to audit, consultation or implementation

Avoid by default:

- no-code DIY framing
- "assemble it yourself" advice
- fake first-person stories
- abstract AI education without a business situation
- western services as everyday default examples for Russia/CIS

## Research Layer Idea

Elements from the "professional content factory" post are useful, but should be adapted.

The immediate useful piece is not a full expensive SEO stack, but a research layer for practical business cases:

- find realistic automation examples
- collect common small-business pains
- track AI news and trends relevant to Russia/CIS
- turn examples into simple posts
- avoid exact numbers unless sourced or explicitly marked as estimates

Future content factory feature:

- research before plan generation
- research before individual post generation
- topic quality filter based on marketing questions
- memory of rejected topics/posts and reasons
- analytics feedback after publication

## Important Product Implication

The content factory should generate plans as a mix of publication roles, not as 30 identical business cases.

It should keep the channel broad enough to be alive, but business-oriented enough to generate trust and leads.
